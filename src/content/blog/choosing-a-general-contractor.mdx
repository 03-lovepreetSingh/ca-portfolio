Hiring a general contractor is one of the biggest decisions you'll make on any
build or renovation. The right partner keeps your project on time, on budget and
stress-free. The wrong one can cost you months and thousands of dollars.

## 1. Verify licensing and insurance

Always confirm the contractor holds the proper provincial licensing and carries
both liability insurance and WSIB (or provincial equivalent) coverage. Ask to
see the certificates — a reputable contractor will provide them without
hesitation.

## 2. Review a real portfolio

Photos are nice, but ask to see projects similar in scope to yours. Better yet,
ask whether you can speak to past clients.

## 3. Get a detailed, written quote

A trustworthy quote breaks down labour, materials, timeline and a clear
change-order process. Vague lump sums are a red flag.

## 4. Understand the payment schedule

Never pay the full amount up front. A fair schedule ties payments to completed
milestones.

> A good contractor earns trust through transparency — at every stage you should
> know exactly where your project and your money stand.

## 5. Check communication early

How quickly and clearly a contractor responds *before* you sign is the best
predictor of how they'll communicate during the build.
